
Pseudo-classes (:):

Selects elements based on their state or position.
Examples:

:hover:
Applies styles when an element is being hovered over by the mouse pointer.

:active:
Applies styles to an element while it is being activated by the user (usually when clicking).

:focus:
Applies styles to an element that currently has keyboard focus.

:first-child:
Selects an element that is the first child of its parent.

:last-child:
Selects an element that is the last child of its parent.

:nth-child(n):
Selects an element that is the nth child of its parent, where n can be a number, a keyword, or a formula.

:nth-last-child(n):
Selects an element that is the nth child counting from the end of its parent's children.

:nth-of-type(n):
Selects an element that is the nth child of its parent of a specific type.

:nth-last-of-type(n):
Selects an element that is the nth child counting from the end of its parent's children of a specific type.

:not(selector):
Selects all elements except those that match the specified selector.

:checked:
Applies styles to an input element that is checked (e.g., checkboxes or radio buttons).

:disabled:
Applies styles to form elements that are disabled.

:required:
Applies styles to form elements that have the required attribute.

:empty:
Selects elements that have no children (including text nodes).

:first-of-type:
Selects the first element of its type among a group of sibling elements.

:last-of-type:
Selects the last element of its type among a group of sibling elements. 
:only-child:
Selects an element that is the only child of its parent.

:only-of-type:
Selects an element that is the only element of its type among a group of sibling elements.

:target:
Applies styles to the targeted element in the URL fragment identifier.
